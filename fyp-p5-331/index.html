intro1

The Five-Year Plan (FYP) is China's top-level policy blueprint. Drawn up every five years since 1953, it sets medium to long term goals for the country's social and economic development. The year of 2020 is the last year of the 13th.

CGTN analyzes how China's development plans have changed over time through comparing the contents and text of China's eight FYPs*, and thus presents a general picture of the People's Republic of China (PRC)'s economic development since its founding.

*The second to sixth FYPs are excluded in this analysis as they are not open to the public.

Task1

Economic development remains the center of each FYP. Content on economic development and industrial restructuring is put on the top list of the targets and tasks for each FYP.

Starting from the seventh, each FYP would set an average annual GDP growth rate, as an important indicator measuring national economy for the next five years.

Task2

From the first FYP, China's industrial structure has experienced dramatic changes with smaller proportion of the primary industry, and bigger shares of the secondary and tertiary industries.

Manufacturing1-show Manufacturing text

During the first FYP, China focused on the development of heavy industry, involving energy, raw material and machinery, and basically formed its own independent industrial system. Data show that the industrial development encountered some setbacks, even negative growth in some years during the second to sixth FYPs.

After the outbreak of SARS in 2003, China included the proposal of establishing a public health emergency response system in its 11th FYP.

When it comes to the 13th FYP, China expanded the public health topic into a whole chapter, mapping out seven aspects to "promote a healthy China."

Since the 10th FYP, the expression of "a country based on the rule of law" replaces "a country with legal system" and the systems of the socialist rule of law, which reflects China's willingness to achieve the overarching goal of modernizing the country's system and capacity for governance through integrating the rule of law.

The phrase "Legal system" focuses more on the static rules and systems of the law while "the rule of law" includes not only dynamic legislative, judicial, administrative law enforcement and law-abiding actions but also a value pursuit, which aims to achieve social fairness and justice, requires limiting public power and safeguarding private rights.

Developing socialist democracy and improve the socialist rule of law is always a key topic for every FYP, whose purpose is to give full expression to the will of the people, protect their rights and interests, spark their creativity, and provide systemic and institutional guarantees to ensure the people run the country.
